The lab was pretty much straight-forward for the most part--it gives you the code to enter and tells you about what it means. Setting things up in the first few steps was pretty easy. Getting through the tutorial itself was very hard. The tutorial is very muddled, long, and even boring to get through. It took far more time than I expected just to get through a few of the steps. 

Fortunately, it wasn't too difficult translating it into my own application. I made an application recording comics that I have. It obviously doesn't have all of them, but comics are one of my special interests. Rebuilding the tutorial around comics was much easier than I had anticipated, with the only hiccups being moving around some pieces of code, renaming some symbols, and certain aspects like hyperlinks not going where they need to. 

Comparing the Jekyll framework to the Razor framework, I found Jekyll's far more straightforward, as pretty much everything revolves around HTML. Razor has you using both HTML and C# intertwined with each other. Thus, Razor's framework revolves around multiple classes. Both frameworks use folders to sort files by their utility, keeping pages and layouts in their appropriate places.

I feel a bit more prepared to create another RazorPages application. It seems much less daunting now that I've had some practice.
